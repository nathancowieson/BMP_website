The history of Betjeman Millenium Park

In 1997 a plot of derelict land was purchased by what is now the Betjeman Millenium Park Trust with planning permission to create a park. The design and layout of the new park was led be a vision to create a semi-natural area consisting of woodland glades linked by a net-work of informal paths. It was envisaged that the Park would:

  • provide a haven for wildlife species, whose populations and diversity would be encouraged to increase
  • be a place of peace and tranquillity for local residents and visitors to enjoy in a variety of ways
  • a space to display excerpts from the work of John Betjeman and encourage visitors to engage with creativity and the Arts

In 1999-2000, with thanks to grants from the Esmee Fairbairn Foundation and Southern Arts, a series of six "Stone Poetry Plaque" sculptures were installed in the park by the artist Alex Peever. The park was officially opened in 2002 and is celebrating its 20th anniversary in May 2022.
